Style Guide

introduction

GoodReads was founded in 2007 by Otis and Elizabeth Chandler, a husband and wife team who wished to help people “find and share books they love.” Using the site’s lists and social networking features, book lovers can find new selections, catalogue, rate and recommend books, and make lists of their favorites to share with others.

The site in its current state includes an extensive number of features which are unwieldy and often hard to find. The existing design also lacks hierarchy in color, typography and graphics. This lack of visual hierarchy amplifies the repetitive nature of listing books and their lengthy metadata, and offers a bland, flat experience for the user.

The goal of this website redesign was to create a more appealing user experience that would integrate two major user goals—finding new books and sharing favorites with others—via the process of cataloguing and sorting books into groups called “shelves.”

Essential to this goal was the creation of an over-arching visual hierarchy to guide the user through the website in a seamless manner. The development of typographic and icon systems and an energetic color palette orders the information in an appealing way, while giving the site a light-hearted feel.

color palette

The primary palette for the GoodReads Website consists of a set of vivid colors which evoke the joyful art of reading. A set of neutral grays and black serve to ground these colors in addition to providing the visual framework for the site.

grid system

The redesign uses a 24-column grid for the large and medium screen formats allowing for maximum flexibility in laying out a variety of content. For smaller screen devices, the grid collapses to a single or double column.

typography

The primary typography for the GoodReads site uses two related font families: Museo Slab and Museo Sans.

Museo Slab, the font used in the creation of the GoodReads wordmark, is a slab-serif font which implies solidity and makes reference to the typewriter, an essential tool of the modern-day writer. It was chosen for its legibility and versatility, along with its sister font, Museo Sans.

wordmark

The GoodReads wordmark (it appears in the header of this style guide) alludes to the written word and the richness of world literature through its typographic reference to the typewriter, essential tool of the modern-era writer. The wordmark appears in black and gray on a white background.

The wordmark always appears with its graphic companion, the multicolored dotted line. The alternating colors of the dotted line run the full page-width along the bottom of the main header bar (as in this style guide), and are not repeated elsewhere on the page. This dotted line graphic is always accompanied by a solid underline in black.

graphic elements

A flexible system of icons, labels and other graphic elements provides clear navigating through the site and enhances the user’s experience.

icons

A set of three icons designate a user’s reading status for any book:

  • finished reading
  • now reading
  • want to read

These clickable icons always appear in their assigned color and with their identifying text labels.

labels

Labels are used to identify sections of interest defined in sidebars and also serve as links to those pages. Examples of labels of this type are the Listopedia and Friends’ Reviews sections. They include black text in Museo Slab 700 on a white background, with a vertically centered black point before and after the text, all framed with a colored 2 pixel rectangular border.

ratings

Ratings are indicated by five gray outlined stars which are filled with orange to indicate a rating for a particular book. If the rating is a consolidation of users’ ratings, the rating’s numerical value is posted to the right of the star rating.

search bars

A search bar is located at the top of each page for site-wide searches. On the My Books page an additional search bar allows for searches within the user’s collection. Search bars are rectangular shaped on the left side and rounded on the right, and are preceded by the word search and a search icon in gray.

images

There are two distinct image treatments in the GoodReads website: one for book cover images and another for portrait photos.

Book cover images are presented with a white margin bordered by a single pixel black line. In “shelf” display, book images maintain a consistent height, with image width fluctuating according to the proportions of the original book dimensions.

Portrait photos of authors (those either writing or being reviewed on the site) and those of GoodReads members both appear in a circular format with a single pixel medium gray border. No margin, drop shadow or other treatment is used.
