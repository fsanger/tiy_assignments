introduction

GoodReads was founded in 2007 by Otis and Elizabeth Chandler, a husband and wife team who wished to help people “find and share books they love.” Using the siteâ€™s lists and social networking features, book lovers can find new selections, catalogue, rate and recommend books, and make lists of their favorites to share with others.

The site in its current state includes an extensive number of features which are unwieldy and often hard to find. The existing design also lacks hierarchy in color, typography and graphics. This lack of visual hierarchy amplifies the repetitive nature of listing books and their lengthy metadata, and offers a bland, flat experience for the user.

The goal of this website redesign was to create a more appealing user experience that would integrate two major user goals—finding new books and sharing favorites with others—via the bridging activity of cataloguing and sorting books into groups called “shelves.”

Essential to this goal was the creation of an overarching visual hierarchy to guide the user through the website in a seamless manner. The development of typographic and icon systems and an energetic color palette orders the information in an appealing way, while giving the site a light-hearted feel.

grid system

The redesign uses a 24-column grid for the large and medium screen formats that allows for maximum flexibility for the layout of a variety of content. For smaller screen devices, the grid collapses to a single or double column.
